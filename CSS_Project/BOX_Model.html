Pizza: A global delight with Italian roots

Flatbreads with toppings have been consumed since ancient times, with mentions in ancient Greece, Rome, and Persia. Modern pizza, specifically Margherita pizza, gained prominence in Naples in the late 18th century, and is credited to Neapolitan pizzaiolo Raffaele Esposito. He created a pizza adorned with tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil to represent the colors of the Italian flag in honor of Queen Margherita of Savoy in 1889. This propelled pizza into the national spotlight and paved the way for its global popularity.
